Who are we?

UniHub is a web application designed to reduce industry academia gap.

What we do?

UniHub provides industrial exposure to software engineering students by providing industrial projects.

What are the key features?

UniHub consists of skill and collaborative experience based team forming, dynamic learning paths based on project description, presentation evaluation with feedback and interview preparation assistant.

Why we do it?

The initial idea of UniHub was for a hackathon and extended as research

How we do it?

By dividing key features into four different components. All these key features are carefully made to produce better industry ready fresh graduates.

Since when?

The brainstorming and defining key features were done from 2021. To extend the knowledge in this area the project was extended as a research project in 2022.

Literature Survey

The following literature reviews are based on the main technologies of UniHub used in different domains.

1.     Team Formation       

Recent studies have identified that the biggest difference between industry and academia is that students at universities can't work together and learn technologies at the level that the industry uses. This gap needs to be closed so that students have the skills and knowledge they need to be successful in their chosen careers. This research aims to find a solution to this problem by focusing on two main questions: how to do industry-based projects in a collaborative working environment and how to form teams that can get the most out of under-skilled students and approach projects by getting students' skills to change over time and working as a team. A Team Formation Problem (TFP) is a way for undergraduates to find a group of people who can do the tasks given. It is a difficult process because you have to make sure that the team you put together can do the job well. The main problem is that when we consider only the number of experts for team formation, the TFP hasn't changed after some time. Then the gap between universities and academia didn’t reduce. But if we give more attention to students who don't have the skills the industry needs, we can train more students and give them to the industry on time in a collaborative working environment. UniHub aims to come up with an algorithm for putting together teams that can work on projects well and give more weight to students with less experience. Also, by keeping an eye on project contributions and analyzing code in an environment where people work together, This could make a big difference in bridging the gap between industry and academia and better preparing students for success in their future careers.  

2.     Project-Based Learning Path  
In addition to programming, it is important to understand algorithms and data structures because they are the building blocks for many modern applications, such as those in artificial intelligence, data analysis, combinatorics, geometry, and numerical analysis. Each of the above-mentioned sub-domains of programming is vast on its own, which could lead to a steep learning curve without proper guidance. Therefore, categorizing educational resources would help programmers decide what they should primarily focus on. With so much information on the internet, it has been hard for users to find the best resource for their needs. Therefore, to overcome this problem, a data filtering system named Recommended System (RS) is specifically designed to find users’ preferences and interests depending on their needs. This part of the research is focused on closing the knowledge gap between academia and industry. This helps to solve one of the biggest problems that students who want to work in the industry face: they don't have enough technical skills from their school projects and work-related projects. To solve this problem, UniHub will focus on creating a learning path with free learning resources that are recommended based on the industry project that the student is working on. This helps to solve one of the biggest problems that students face when they want to work in the industry.  

3.     Presentation Evaluation and Feedback ·
A project that has been effectively finished must be presented to management or clients. Therefore, presentation skills are essential for professionals. A presentation should be simple, understandable, and delivered in an interesting way to the audience to grab their attention. However, delivering a good presentation can be challenging and stressful for many people. This conveys the necessity of improving one’s presentation skills in an effective and reliable way. To bridge this gap in presentation skills, the study aims to improve undergraduate students’ presentation skills before graduation. As an approach to mitigating the problem, the study will focus on evaluating presentation videos to provide feedback on how to improve. This system offers undergraduate students an innovative and systematic approach to improving their presentation skills.  
4.     Interview Preparation Assistant 
Interviews are the most important and necessary part of the hiring process for a candidate. Every company wants to complete a project successfully, and the key to doing so is to hire a highly trained workforce. As the competition increases, many companies implement tough selection procedures to filter out the most skilled candidates. The companies expect candidates to possess not only technical skills but also communication and behavioral skills. However, many candidates fall short of the industry expectations as they can't impress the interviewers during the interview.

Research Problem

One of the recurring problems in information technology (IT) is the gap between education and industry. The gap takes several forms including difficulty in technology transfer, aligning research with the needs of practitioners, and preparing students to make the most effective contribution upon entering the industry. Graduating computer science and software engineering students do not always possess the necessary skills, abilities, or knowledge when beginning their careers in the software industry. The lack of these skills and abilities can limit the productivity of newly hired, and recent graduates, or even prevent them from gaining employment. Academic institutions and industries are performing on their own terms without little or no coordination and collaboration. And as a consequence of that, academia ends up producing graduates or a workforce that industry cannot engage to advance their cause for the common good of all. Industry on its part cannot produce or manufacture more because they lack the needed workforce to accommodate expansion. And in most cases, the nation bears the consequential loses that result from such a conundrum. ‘NATIONAL IT-BPM WORKFORCE SURVEY 2019’ conducted by ICTA Sri Lanka has identified that there is a skill gap of the IT professionals produced by Sri Lankan Universities. By examining the recent research, we identified several requirements that should be fulfilled to bridge the industry-academia gap. The main skill gaps we identified are as follows, ü  Lack of presentation skills ü  Lack of communications skills, teamwork, and project management. ü  Lack of technical skill to complete an industry project ü  Lack of interview-facing skills These skill gap issue should be resolved to produce the most effective workforce and meet the expectations of the industry.

Methodologies

1.     Team Formation  
The Team formation is to provide industry exposure to the undergraduate students by allowing them to collaboratively work on real enterprise projects. Companies are allowed to submit project proposals with test cases to the students through the app. Analyze the project description using NLP and identify the students to whom the project is relevant. Classify the identified students based on their skills and experience. Social network graph and cluster-based approach for generating the most effective teams for a particular project. Identify community leaders from social network graph. Based on position scores, roles will be assigned. For the evaluating process, the monitoring process is done through GitHub and using the code quality analyzer tool to assign score value for the project. Generate a Leaderboard for the relevant project

2.     Project-Based Learning Path 
The Project-Based Learning Path with free learning resources for undergraduate and fresh graduate students to complete their technical assessments. Initially students can log in to provided UniHub solution and they can access the Project-Based Learning Path feature. In that feature, they can provide their project description of the technical assessment. The received project description will be analyzed using NER and Intent Identification based on approach using NLP. The project requirements will be identified from the analyzed project description. The project requirements from the project description such as “web application”, “front-end”, “back-end”, etc.… The identified project requirement will be used to carry out web scraping. Custom Search API will be used to extract free learning resources from the websites and YouTube API will be used to extract free learning resources from YouTube. Collecting the free learning resources using web scraping and then it will be analyzed for the most recommended free learning resources through the identified project requirements using sentiment analysis.  

3.     Presentation Evaluation and Feedback  

4.     Interview Preparation Assistant An Interview preparation assistant which allows the users to have mock interviews and prepare well for the real interview. Initially when the student logs in to the UniHub app, they can access the Interview Preparation Assistant feature. After accessing this feature, students are required to upload the CV and the Job description. The keywords are extracted from the CV and the Job description using NLP. SpaCy library will be used for this process. NER and Intent Identification based approach will be used to identify job requirements. The user can initiate a mock interview session with a virtual interviewer. The interview consists of two parts which are Candidate understanding and Technical assessment. In the Candidate understanding phase, candidates will be asked about themselves, their experiences, projects they have done etc. In this phase, follow-up questions are generated based on the response of the candidate using the Questgen library. In the Technical assessment phase, candidates will be asked questions to check their technical knowledge. The questions are recommended based on the skills mentioned in the CV and the job requirements. The database contains the previous interview questions and answers. A question recommendation system will be built using NLP techniques such as TF-IDF and cosine similarity. The answers of the candidate are matched with the answers in the database, and a score is generated. Facial expressions of the candidate will be detected using face-api.js a JavaScript framework. This is done in the front end to reduce the stress on the backend servers, and the collected data will be sent to the backend for further evaluation. Eventually, the candidate is given feedback on their performance, and a report with improvement guidance is generated. Improvement criteria and guidelines will be obtained from the trusted and personalized self-improvement recommendations will be given to the candidate.

Recent studies show that there is a big gap between industry and academia in the field of software engineering. This means that undergraduates often don't have the knowledge and skills that companies are looking for. When they first start working, undergraduates from our schools often don't meet the standards set by the industry, and most of these undergraduates don't have any skills. In order to make them a part of the industry, extra resources were often needed to give them some internal education. Most of these companies in these industries have had to teach graduates the skills and knowledge they need to do their jobs well. Even though many computer science and software engineering classes require students to do practical assignments, these tasks are usually pointless and don't help students grow as professionals. Because of this, many college graduates don't have the skills that employers want and are turned down for interviews. This research paper looks for and investigates ways to teach university students how to work together, give presentations, use technology, and do well in interviews. One of the hardest things for undergraduates is that they don't have the knowledge and skills they need to work on a real industrial project. Some of the problems we discovered from our surveys included a lack of interviewing skills and a lack of presentation abilities. The majority of undergraduates also have trouble locating reliable resources for learning new skills. Thus, our main goal is to develop a (UniHub) platform that overcomes the aforementioned problems and gives undergraduates virtual work experience so they may get ready for the workforce before they graduate.

Implement an interactive application to reduce the industry-academia gap by providing them with real industry exposure and assisting them in improving both technical and soft skills. Provide personalized skill development strategies for each student by monitoring them individually to uplift the skillset irrespective of their academic performance. The ultimate goal is to create industry-ready fresh graduates.

UniHub is an advanced community platform that utilizes a variety of technologies and frameworks such as Deep Learning, Machine Learning, Deep Neural Networks, Social Network Analysis, and graph-based algorithms. These technologies are used to perform tasks such as team formation. Additionally, several CNN models and the GPT-3 language model, as well as Natural Language Processing techniques like Named Entity Recognition, are used in our interview preparation bot. Image processing, audio processing, and Google Tesseract are also used in our presentation evaluation models. We are also adding several new features to our platform such as a recommendation engine that uses advanced pre-trained models like BERT and several NLP techniques. We also run community detection algorithms for faster decision-making in our high-volume platform. For example, our Team Formation algorithm. For companies, we provide clusters of the best suitable students for their events by using different clustering techniques. In the future, we plan to implement more latest technologies to improve our platform since we have a lot of data available. This will allow us to continue providing an innovative and valuable service to our users.
